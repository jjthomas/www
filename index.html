Research Reports and Presentations

  1. For my M. Eng., I have been working on NVL, an intermediate language for data parallel computations that targets vector units, multicores, and GPU's to achieve machine-level performance. Here is a poster describing our work so far. It was presented at the BigData@CSAIL poster session on 11/5/2015.
  2. As part of the SuperUROP undergraduate research program at MIT, which I completed in the Supertech group, and the spring 2015 research course 6.886, I worked on a graph-parallel computation framework designed to run physical simulations on locally connected 3D mesh graphs. A paper on this work, which is still ongoing, can be found here.
  3. During the first half of the SuperUROP program, I worked on a tool for low-overhead collection of work and critical path length statistics from Cilk programs. A poster on this work that I presented at a SuperUROP poster session in fall 2014 can be found here.

Class Reports

This project was recognized as one of the best in the course at the annual EECS awards ceremony. The project writeup can be found here.
